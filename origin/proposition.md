# 🌿 Saison — Proposition

> *"Ce que la terre donne, elle le donne à son heure."*
> An open-source, seasonal produce discovery and gardening guide.

---

## Vision

Saison connects people to the natural rhythm of the earth — through what they eat and what they grow. It is not a database with a skin. It is a living calendar: the interface shifts with the seasons, the colours breathe with the months, and every interaction feels grounded in the physical world.

Most existing tools in this space are either cluttered spreadsheets or corporate green wellness apps. Saison is neither. It is a **field journal made digital** — precise, beautiful, and deeply seasonal.

---

## Design Decisions

### Aesthetic Direction

**"Botanical Modernism"** — the structural clarity of modernist design, given life by the visual language of botanical illustration and the rhythm of the seasons.

This means:
- **Structure from modernist principles** — clear hierarchy, intentional whitespace, typographic discipline, and a consistent spatial rhythm. The layout is governed by CSS Grid, but the grid is *curated*, not uniform. Featured items span wider; herbs and flowers sit smaller. The page feels composed, not generated.
- **Soul from the botanical tradition** — serif headings that evoke a plant encyclopedia, earthy colour palettes that shift with the calendar, hand-drawn SVG illustrations for every produce entry, and a faint paper-like texture on surfaces to soften the digital flatness.
- **Life from the seasonal system** — the colour transitions as you navigate through months are the heartbeat of the interface. This is not decoration; it is the primary experience.

> [!info] Why not "Organic Bauhaus"?
> The original label created a contradiction — "organic" promised warmth and irregularity, but "Bauhaus" delivered rigid uniformity. "Botanical Modernism" resolves this: it keeps the discipline (grid, type hierarchy, clear spacing) while replacing the cold precision with the texture and warmth of a hand-illustrated field guide.

### Layout Philosophy: The Curated Grid

The grid is the backbone, but it should feel **composed by a human**, not generated by an algorithm.

- **CSS Grid with varied spans** — peak-season or featured items occupy `2×1` cells; herbs and small entries sit at `1×1`. Category headers break the grid into meaningful sections ("Stone Fruits", "Root Vegetables", "Leafy Greens"). The result feels like the pages of a well-designed cookbook — structured but alive.
- **Responsive rhythm** — `1 column` on small mobile, `2 columns` on large mobile / small tablet, `3 columns` on tablet, `4 columns` on desktop. The layout breathes at every breakpoint rather than cramming a fixed grid into small screens.
- **Grouped by category** — produce is not dumped into a flat list. Items are grouped under soft section headings with subtle dividers, giving the page chapters like a journal.
- **Staggered card entrance** — cards appear with a gentle cascade (staggered `animation-delay`, 40–60ms per card, short opacity+translateY), giving the feeling of things growing into the page.

### The Signature Interaction: The Wheel

Inspired by the circular calendar found at *lesfruitsetlegumesfrais.com*, the centrepiece of Saison is a **seasonal wheel** — a circular month navigator at the top of every page.

The wheel:
- Shows all 12 months arranged in a circle.
- The **current month is highlighted** and visually elevated.
- Rotating the wheel (click/drag/touch) moves through months.
- As the selected month changes, the **entire page palette transitions** — a slow, living shift in background and accent colour. This is the one animation we invest in heavily.
- Prev/Next arrow buttons flank it for accessibility.
- On mobile, the wheel **scales down gracefully** (reduced radius, abbreviated month labels) but remains the primary navigation — it does not collapse into a dropdown.

This is not decoration. It is the primary navigation element of the entire app.

### Colour System (Seasonal Tokens)

CSS custom properties are set on `:root` and overridden via a `data-season` attribute on `<body>`. The palette is not just four blocks — **each month has a unique blend** that interpolates between its season and the next, giving the transitions a continuous, organic quality rather than hard cuts at season boundaries.

### Typography

A deliberate pairing that evokes a botanical journal:

- **Display / Headings** — a refined serif: *DM Serif Display* (or *Playfair Display*, *Lora*). Used for the logo, page titles, section headings, and the produce name on detail pages. This carries the "printed reference book" feeling.
- **Body / Labels / Data** — a neutral, modern sans: *DM Sans* (or *Outfit*, *Inter*). Used for category badges, meta text, descriptions, and all UI controls. Keeps things legible and contemporary.
- **No monospace, no novelty fonts** — the type system stays tight: two families, strict size scale, consistent letter-spacing.

### Surface & Texture

To prevent the flat, generic "SaaS dashboard" look:

- **Faint paper texture** — a CSS-only noise pattern (`background-image` with a subtle SVG filter or a tiny repeating data-URI) applied to card surfaces and the main background. Barely visible, but it gives warmth under the fingers.
- **Soft shadows over hard borders** — cards use a very light `box-shadow` rather than heavy borders. Edges feel soft, like printed paper laid on a table.
- **Muted colour swatches** — each produce card has a thin swatch strip derived from its `colors` field, but the swatches are desaturated slightly to sit comfortably within the earthy palette.

## Tech Stack & Architecture

### Runtime & Backend
- **Bun** — runtime, package manager, test runner, bundler. Single tool, zero overhead. Bun 1.3+ gives us `bun:sqlite` natively and the unified `Bun.SQL` API.
- **SvelteKit** — full-stack framework. Handles routing, SSR, API endpoints, and static prerendering in one package. No separate backend server needed for MVP.
  - Server routes (`+page.server.ts`) query SQLite directly using `bun:sqlite`.
  - No client-side API calls needed for the core seasonal view — HTML arrives fully rendered via SSR.
  - Hybrid rendering: produce data pages are prerendered at build time; the wheel interaction is client-side.
- **SQLite** via `bun:sqlite` — local-first, zero-dependency, the DB file is a single artifact that can be committed or distributed. Synchronous API for optimal performance.

> [!info] Why SvelteKit over Elysia + React?
> For this project's scope — content-heavy, mostly read-only, open source — SvelteKit gives routing, SSR, and API routes in a single framework with minimal boilerplate. Svelte 5's compiler produces near-zero runtime overhead, which aligns directly with the "fast, minimal" mandate. ElysiaJS will be introduced in Phase 4 if a standalone public API (for mobile or third-party access) becomes necessary.

### Frontend
- **Svelte 5 + SvelteKit** — file-based routing, SSR by default, built-in transitions.
  - **Runes API** — all state management uses `$state()`, `$derived()`, and `$props()` (no legacy `$:` or `export let`). State files use `.svelte.ts` extension for universal reactivity.
  - **Snippets** over slots — `{#snippet}` / `{@render}` for composable markup.
  - **Event handlers as props** — `onclick={handler}` pattern, no `createEventDispatcher`.
- **Vanilla CSS with custom properties** — no Tailwind. Keeps contributor friction low and gives full control over the hand-crafted aesthetic. One global `tokens.css`, one `reset.css`, component-scoped styles in `.svelte` files.
- **No UI component library** — every component is bespoke and part of the design system.

### Data Layer
- **Source of truth: Markdown files with YAML frontmatter** in `/data/produce/`.
- A `bun run seed` script parses these into SQLite at build time.
- Contributors submit a PR adding a single `.md` file — no database GUI, no migrations, no code required.

## Data Architecture

### YAML Frontmatter Format (`/data/produce/tomato.md`)

```yaml
---
id: tomato
name: Tomato
name_fr: Tomate
category: fruit
featured: true           # spans 2 columns when in season
description: >
  The tomato thrives in heat and full sun. Sweet, acidic, and essential to
  summer cooking across the Mediterranean and beyond.
season_start: 6
season_end: 9
planting_start: 3
planting_end: 5
harvest_start: 7
harvest_end: 9
hemisphere: northern
zone_offset: 0
colors: ["#C0392B", "#E74C3C", "#922B21"]
companions: [basil, marigold, parsley]
avoid: [fennel, brassicas]
illustration: tomato.svg  # path to botanical SVG in /static/illustrations/
---

Extended prose description, growing notes, culinary uses, etc.
```

### Database Schema

```sql
CREATE TABLE produce (
  id             TEXT PRIMARY KEY,
  name           TEXT NOT NULL,
  name_fr        TEXT,
  category       TEXT CHECK(category IN ('fruit','vegetable','herb','flower')),
  featured       INTEGER DEFAULT 0,   -- boolean: spans wider in grid
  description    TEXT,
  season_start   INTEGER NOT NULL,    -- 1–12
  season_end     INTEGER NOT NULL,
  planting_start INTEGER,
  planting_end   INTEGER,
  harvest_start  INTEGER,
  harvest_end    INTEGER,
  hemisphere     TEXT DEFAULT 'northern',
  zone_offset    INTEGER DEFAULT 0,
  colors         TEXT,  -- JSON array of hex strings
  companions     TEXT,  -- JSON array of produce IDs
  avoid          TEXT,  -- JSON array of produce IDs
  illustration   TEXT   -- filename of SVG illustration
);

CREATE INDEX idx_season ON produce(season_start, season_end);
CREATE INDEX idx_category ON produce(category);
```

## Views & Layout

### 1. The Wheel — `/` (Landing / Current Month)

- Full-width circular month wheel at the top, centred.
- Below: **curated, category-grouped grid** of produce cards for the selected month.
  - Items are grouped under soft section headings (e.g., "Fruits", "Vegetables", "Herbs").
  - Featured items (`featured: true`) span 2 columns.
  - Each card shows: botanical SVG illustration, name, category badge, colour swatch strip.
- Page background and accent colour transition smoothly as the wheel turns.
- On mobile: wheel scales down; grid collapses to 1–2 columns; category groups stack vertically.

### 2. The Detail Card — `/produce/[id]`

- Hero band in the produce's dominant colour, with the botanical illustration displayed prominently.
- **Temporal Map**: a horizontal SVG timeline showing the 12-month year with coloured bands for planting window, growing period, and harvest window.
- Prose description from the Markdown body.
- Companion planting grid (Phase 3+).

### 3. The Archive — `/month/[1-12]`

- Same curated grid layout as landing, but for any selected month.
- Accessible via the wheel navigation and direct URL.

### 4. The Planner — `/plan` (Phase 2)

- "What should I plant this month?" filtered view.
- Toggle between Sow / Tend / Harvest views.

## Naming

**Saison** is the strongest choice. It is:
- Simple and pronounceable in French and English.
- Timeless — it does not over-explain.
- Evocative of the core premise without being literal.
- Works as a domain, an npm package, a GitHub repo, and a brand.

Runner-up: **Terroir** — beautiful, but risks feeling wine-specific.

## Accessibility & Open Source

- Full keyboard navigation for the wheel (arrow keys, Enter to select).
- `aria-label` and `aria-current` on all navigation states.
- Colour contrast maintained across all seasonal palettes (WCAG AA minimum).
- Reduced motion support: `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` disables transitions and card entrance animations.
- YAML schema documented in `CONTRIBUTING.md` so non-developers can submit produce data via PR.
- MIT licence.
